Charity Drawing

Please note that all tea party attendees are automatically entered in the charity drawing. Please do not fill out the drawing form if you are attending the tea party.

We will hold a charity drawing to benefit the Equal Justice Initiative. We will announce the winners at the Royal Vegas Online closing ceremonies. You must submit your entries by November 15 at 2:00 PM PT.

No monetary purchase is necessary to enter or win the drawing. Any attempted donations directly to PPC will not result in a contest entry and will be promptly refunded. Please note that the drawing is only open to United States residents. It is not open to residents of Alabama, Hawaii, Kansas, New Jersey, and Utah, in compliance with applicable state laws.

You can enter our drawing by 1. Registering for the Tea Party or Tea Party waitlist, 2. Submitting proof of donation of $5 or more to the Equal Justice Initiative, or 3. reposting our informational Instagram post (see this section for more information). Limit one entry per person.

We will choose winners by random drawing, and each winner will receive one of the following: a $125 gift card from BB & B, a Fluffy Tori bundle (pin of your choice, sticker sheet, coord planner notepad), a bath bundle from Twootie Tarte, or a PPC Merch Shop swag bundle.

Enter by filling out the charity drawing entry form starting October 10. Please do not fill out this form if you have registered for the Tea Party or Tea Party waitlist.

How to Enter the RVO Giveaway Using Instagram

This post is aimed at people who want to fight racial injustice in their communities but don't know how to start. Image provided by @prettyprincess.club
* Get involved in local politics: Is your city council voting to defund the police or fund community aid efforts? Find the city councilmember for your district and call, email, or snail-mail them to encourage them to pledge their support.
* Donate Money: To organizations like the Equal Justice Initiative or directly to people affected by injustice.
* Donate Goods: Your local mutual aid organization might have a list of specific goods they need to support their community. Check their current needs, mask up, and go shopping!
* Educate Your Friends and Family: Find ways to discuss your role in combating racial injustice with your people every day.
